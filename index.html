About Us

Wolfbyte FTC is a robotics team based in Santa Clara County, competing in the First Tech Challenge. We are a group of passionate students who design, build, and program robots to take on challenging competitions. Our team is dedicated to pushing the boundaries of engineering and software development using CAD, 3D printing, and Java-based coding.

Beyond competition, we are committed to STEM outreach and mentorship. We engage with our community, inspire younger students, and collaborate with industry professionals to advance robotics education. We believe in teamwork, perseverance, and creativity to tackle real-world challenges through technology.


What is FTC?

FTC stands for FIRST Tech Challenge, a robotics competition for middle and high school students that blends the thrill of sports with the rigors of science and technology. In FTC, teams of students design, build, and program robots to compete in an annual game that changes every season. The robots are typically small, no larger than 18 inches on each side, and are controlled using a combination of pre-written code and driver input through gamepads. Teams also document their engineering process, work on community outreach, and often collaborate with mentors and sponsors.
